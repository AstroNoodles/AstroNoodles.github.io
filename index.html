Michael Batavia is a student at the Bronx High School of Science who is an advanced programmer in Java, C++ and Python. Michael has shown his programming commitment through his perfect attendance as a member of the Bronx Science Hackers Club and is the current president of the Bronx Science Machine Learning Club. He has created several research projects under the supervision of Dr. Shapovalov and Stony Brook professor Dr. Yuefan Deng. You can see his project here.  Additionally featured in the Skills, he created a specialized convolutional neural network to diagnose breast cancer effectively and improves on previous experts' limitations by asynchronizing I/O and parallelizing across multiple GPUs. He is also a member of the non-profit TeenHacksLI and effectively designed workshops, advocated for the event in his school and obtained partnerships with leading start-ups in computer science. He is also interested in biochemistry, classical and modern physics through his contribution in the Reactions physical science journal at his school. He is currently a senior editor in the magazine.
